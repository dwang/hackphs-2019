What defines a good posture?

This is the ideal posture position. You may not like it, but this is peak, perfect posture. At first, you may feel sore; however, this is healthier for your body.


What happens if you continue having bad posture?

Some possible effects include:

  • Increased muscle strain and risk for injury
  • Back pain
  • Muscle fatigue
  • Headaches
  • And much more...

What does your score mean?

It's just a numerical represention of your current posture. It is calculated by comparing your calibration settings when you configured the device, and your current sitting position.

Ideally, the lower score you have, the better it is for your health.

For example, a score of 50 is nearly perfect, as compared to a score of 160 which means the posture needs a lot of improvement.
